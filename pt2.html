IWA

For my IWA/Individual Written Assignment, I found it much more challenging to fill my writing without making it drag or repeat itself. I wanted to keep the information concise, as the topic is very sensitive, and bringing personal emotions into it may change the credibility of it. I wrote about the effects of AI companions on human connection, as many people have begun to turn to them to help combat loneliness or boredom. As someone who wants to see AI develop in a good way, to be used as a tool rather than a crutch, I personally feel that the development of AI companions is inherently harmful, but I had to keep an open mind. While my emotions had shown to be "correct" in a sense, as AI companions are programmed to manipulate their users, I was also shown that AI companions can be helpful as a non-personal friend that suggests ideas and ways to cope with certain mental disorders.

Presenting my personal project was a lot more nerve-racking and scary for me. I'm going to open up here, and admit that I suffer from general anxiety disorder and ADHD, so it was pretty hard for me to sit still, let alone not be terrified of presenting the incredibly sensitive and unique topic I was writing about. That's why I decided to make my slides as good as I possibly could, by adding subtle symbolisms and metaphors into the backgrounds, colors, and layouts to increase the depth in emotion and information. When it came to presenting though, I also needed to ensure that I would stutter and forget my slides as little as I possibly could. To do this, I decided to take a different approach to memorizing my slides, which was not to have them out, loaded on a device, or printed, at all until I had gone through the entire slideshow in my head. In the end, that tactic worked very well for me, and I will continue to use it to study.
